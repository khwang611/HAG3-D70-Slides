What it finds

Missing side with SAS

When you know two sides and the included angle, Law of Cosines finds the side across from that angle.

c² = a² + b² − 2ab cos(C)

Why not Law of Sines?

You need a complete opposite pair.

Law of Sines needs an angle and its opposite side. In SAS and SSS, you do not start with a complete opposite pair, so Law of Cosines is the better tool.

Classwork Example

The swamp question: building the Law of Cosines

Scenario: Surveyors are measuring a triangular plot of land with a swamp in the middle. Two sides are known: 120 ft and 200 ft. The included angle is 41°. The swamp makes the third side hard to measure, so we calculate it.

Given

Side 1 = 120 ftSide 2 = 200 ftIncluded angle = 41°

Step 1: Identify the case.

This is SAS because we know two sides and the included angle. That means Law of Cosines works.

Step 2: Write the formula.

x² = 120² + 200² − 2(120)(200)cos(41°)

Step 3: Simplify.

x² = 14,400 + 40,000 − 48,000cos(41°)

Step 4: Calculate.

x² ≈ 18,175.59

x ≈ √18,175.59 ≈ 134.82

Final Answer

The missing side is approximately 134.8 feet.

Classwork Strategy

How to use Law of Cosines.

Identify what is given.

Look for SAS or SSS. For SAS, make sure the angle is between the two known sides.

Choose the correct formula.

If finding side c, use c² = a² + b² − 2ab cos(C).

Substitute carefully.

The angle in cosine must be the included angle across from the missing side.

Take the square root.

After solving for the squared side, square root to get the actual side length.

Problem 1: Swamp / plot of land

Two sides of a triangular plot are 120 ft and 200 ft. The included angle is 41°. Find the missing side.

Write the formula.

x² = 120² + 200² − 2(120)(200)cos(41°)

Simplify the squares and product.

x² = 14,400 + 40,000 − 48,000cos(41°)

Evaluate.

x² ≈ 18,175.59

Square root.

x ≈ 134.82

Answer: The missing side is about 134.8 ft.

Problem 2: Soccer field

Sierra is 16.8 m from the center of the goal and 17.1 m from Alex. The included angle is 49.6°. Find the distance from Alex to the center of the goal.

Identify SAS.

The two known distances meet at Sierra, and the included angle is 49.6°.

Write the formula.

x² = 16.8² + 17.1² − 2(16.8)(17.1)cos(49.6°)

Simplify.

x² = 282.24 + 292.41 − 574.56cos(49.6°)

Evaluate.

x² ≈ 202.03

Square root.

x ≈ 14.21

Answer: Alex is about 14.2 meters from the center of the goal.

Problem 3: Expressing Law of Cosines in different ways

For a triangle with sides a, b, and c, each formula finds the side across from the matching angle.

a² = b² + c² − 2bc cos(A)

b² = a² + c² − 2ac cos(B)

c² = a² + b² − 2ab cos(C)

How to remember it

The side by itself on the left matches the angle inside cosine. For example, c² uses cos(C).
